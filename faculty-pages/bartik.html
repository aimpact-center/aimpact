Alex Bartik is an Assistant Professor of Economics at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign. He studies how new policies, tools, and technologies impact people, labor markets, and cities. Much of his research involves collecting novel data through field experiments, surveys, and new measurement approaches.

Published Work

My work related to this (providing ML based job search recommendations, using LLMs to measure regulations, cash transfers) are all working papers and not published yet.

Relevant Projects

The impact of personalized job recommendations: This is an experiment with Allegheny County Department of Human Services where we built a job search tool combining user preferences with ML based predictions of utility in different jobs to provide job recommendations. We'll have new results this year.

The Costs of Housing Regulations: Evidence from Generative Regulatory Measurement: We use LLMs to develop new measures of housing regulations nationwide.

Open Research Unconditional income Study: The largest privately funded unconditional cash transfer study in the united states. Provides evidence on how unconditional cash transfers affect individual outcomes. Unconditional cash transfers are one policy some have proposed to respond to labor market disruptions due to AI.
