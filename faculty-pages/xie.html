Shihan Xie is an Assistant Professor of Economics at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign. Her research lies at the intersection of behavioral macroeconomics and monetary economics, focusing on how individuals form beliefs about the macroeconomy and how these beliefs shape their responses to macroeconomic news and policies. Her work often involves designing and running original survey experiments to capture how real-world expectations influence economic decisions.
