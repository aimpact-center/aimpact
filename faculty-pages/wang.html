Dong Wang

Professor of Information Sciences
Faculty Affiliate

Website / dwang24@illinois.edu

Dong Wang is a Professor in the School of Information Sciences and School of Computing and Data Science (affiliated) at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign. His research interests lie in the area of social sensing, intelligence and computing, human-centered AI, and big data analytics. His research has resulted in computing systems and software tools with applications in academia, industry, and government research labs. He has authored three books: Social Intelligence (Springer, 2025), Social Edge Computing (Springer, 2023), and Social Sensing (Elsevier, 2015). He is the recipient of an NSF CAREER Award, Google Faculty Research Award, ARO Young Investigator Program (YIP), Best Paper Awards from ASONAM 2022 and IEEE RTAS, and a Best Paper Honorable Mention from ACM CHI 2025 and IEEE SmartComp 2022. He serves as Associate Editor of IEEE Transactions on Big Data, Frontiers in Big Data, and the Social Network Analysis and Mining Journal, and is an IEEE Senior Member and ACM and AAAI Member.

Relevant Projects

Project 1: AI for Smart and Safe Drinking Water
Monitoring drinking water contamination is vitally important to inform consumers about water safety, identify source water problems, and facilitate discussion of public health and the environment. The overall goal of this project is to develop a framework for reliable and timely detection of drinking water contamination through AI modeling and citizen science.

Project 2: AI for Social Drought
Droughts are increasingly recognized as environmental crises with far-reaching consequences beyond water availability. This project develops a novel socially informed AI-driven drought estimation framework that estimates both drought severity and its societal impact using data from social and news media.

Project 3: AI for Metacognitive Calibration in Education
Students often have difficulty estimating how well they know a topic, leading to inefficient learning or suboptimal educational outcomes. This project aims to improve students’ ability to estimate their knowledge in the learning process using AI methods.
