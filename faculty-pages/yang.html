Nathan Yang

Associate Professor and C.W. Faculty Fellow
Faculty Affiliate

Website / ncyang@illinois.edu

Nathan Yang is an Associate Professor and C.W. Faculty Fellow at the Gies College of Business, University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign. He received his PhD in Economics from the University of Toronto. His research interests are in behavioral analytics and retail strategy, and has been published in marketing (Marketing Science, Journal of Marketing Research, Management Science, Quantitative Marketing and Economics), economics (Quantitative Economics), and computer science (WWW). He has also served on the editorial review board for Marketing Science and Journal of Experimental Psychology: Applied.

Relevant Projects

Luo, Lijuan, Emaad Manzoor, and Nathan Yang (2025), “Creative Marketplaces for Humans & Machines” (working paper)
We study creative marketplaces where consumers can either purchase human-created content or generate customized content using an integrated AI system. This model allows us to highlight a central trade-off in generative AI markets: while compensation ensures that creators are rewarded for their data, it does not systematically improve efficiency and may even harm consumers.

Rao, Vithala, Nathan Yang, and Saleh Zakerinia (2025), “Using Quantum Game Theory to Model Competition”
This paper offers a practitioners guide to using quantum game theory for studying competition between agents.
