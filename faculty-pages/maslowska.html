Ewa Maslowska

Associate Professor of Advertising
Faculty Affiliate

ehm@illinois.edu

Ewa Maslowska, Ph.D., is an Associate Professor in the Charles H. Sandage Department of Advertising at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign, with affiliate appointments in the Institute of Communications Research, Informatics, and the Center for Social & Behavioral Science. She holds a Ph.D. in Communication Science from the University of Amsterdam and an M.A. in Psychology from Jagiellonian University and completed postdoctoral research at the Spiegel Research Center at Northwestern University. Ewa specializes in personalized persuasive communication. Her research examines how individuals engage with digital platforms, focusing on the persuasive effects of technologies such as recommendation algorithms, platform design features, and AI-driven content delivery. Her recent work explores the psychological dimensions of human-AI interaction, particularly the formation of relationships with conversational agents and the implications of empathy outsourcing.

Relevant Projects

My current research projects try to understand if people form (impressions of) relationships with AI chatbots as anecdotal and initial research evidence suggests, and if so, how that happens and what are the consequences. Can these chatbots be persuasive? What does it do to our emotional skills and our sense of self?

1. Human-AI Relationship Development
A longitudinal study tracking how people develop relationships with AI chatbots over time using experience sampling. Currently developing the design.

2. Persuasiveness of AI Chatbots
A randomized controlled experiment comparing the persuasiveness of personalized and non-personalized AI chatbots. Currently revising the IRB application (round 2).

3. Anthropomorphisation of AI Chatbots
Observation with interviews building on Psychological Attachment Theory to understand what communication and design elements cue people to humanize AI chatbots. IRB submitted.

4. The Effects of Empathy Outsourcing to AI Chatbots and Moral Deskilling
Building on the Communicate Bond Belong theory, two lab experiments using psychophysiological measures compare empathy engagement vs empathy outsourcing. Preproposal accepted by CSBS. Currently developing a full proposal.

5. Algorithms and Self
Interviews to understand how recommendation algorithms reflect and affect individuals’ self, with plans to extend to the effects of AI chatbots on self.
