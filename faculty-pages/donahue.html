Kate Donahue

Incoming Assistant Professor of Computer Science
Faculty Affiliate

Website / kpd@illinois.edu

Kate Donahue is a METEOR postdoc at MIT, working with Manish Raghavan, and a member of the AI for Society working group. Starting in summer 2026, she will be an Assistant Professor of Computer Science at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign. She works on algorithmic problems relating to the societal impact of AI, including fairness, human/AI collaboration, and game-theoretic models of federated and collaborative learning. She completed her Ph.D. in Computer Science at Cornell, advised by Jon Kleinberg. She has been selected as a “Rising Star in EECS” and her PhD research was supported by an NSF Graduate Research Fellowship.

Relevant Projects

Human-AI Communication Games
How can AI tools optimally query humans in order to satisfy their preferences, given imperfect information?

Multi-agent LLM Aggregation
Given a fixed way that humans aggregate information from multiple LLMs, what are the implications for a “marketplace” of LLMs that has good impacts for humans (e.g., heterogeneity or homogeneity?)

Incentives in Data Sharing
How do different degrees of privacy preferences and/or decentralized levels of coordination affect incentives to share data for overall societal welfare?

Medical Data
Can we use medical data to understand settings when human doctors may have more information than is encoded in AI standard of care?
