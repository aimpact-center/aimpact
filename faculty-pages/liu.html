Jingnan Liu

Assistant Professor of Economics
Faculty Affiliate

Website / jingnan@illinois.edu

Jingnan Liu is an Assistant Professor of Economics at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign and affiliated faculty at MIT FutureTech. Her research spans macroeconomics, labor economics, economic growth, innovation, and firm dynamics. She is particularly interested in how technology shapes the economy, how we can accelerate technological progress, and how firm dynamics interact with labor market outcomes. She received her Ph.D. in Economics from the University of Wisconsin–Madison and was previously a Postdoctoral Associate at MIT Sloan School of Management and the MIT Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence Laboratory (CSAIL).

Published Work

At the moment, AI-related projects are ongoing and have not yet reached the publication stage.

Relevant Projects

Project I: AI and Science
This project examines how AI impacts science and the broader economy. Key topics include quantifying the impact of AI on scientific progress and economic growth, the extent to which AI will augment and/or change the R&D production function, and the implications of AI-driven R&D for researchers, firms, and policy.

Project II: AI and the Labor Market
This project investigates how AI is reshaping the labor market, with a focus on the supply of AI talent. We ask: Who enters AI jobs? Who benefits from wage premiums? How quickly do workers reskill or switch into AI jobs? And to what extent is there misallocation in the labor market?
