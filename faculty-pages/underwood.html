Ted Underwood

Professor of Information Sciences and English
Founding Member and Faculty Affiliate

Website / tunder@illinois.edu

Ted Underwood is Professor of Information Sciences and English at UIUC. His research explores two interrelated questions: how AI can help us better understand cultural history, and how we can use historical training data to produce AI models that do a better job of understanding pluralism and reasoning about different cultural perspectives.

Published Work

T. Underwood, “The Impact of Language Models on the Humanities, and Vice Versa,” Nature Computational Sciences, July 2025.

T. Underwood, “Mapping the Latent Spaces of Culture,” Startwords, August 2022.

T. Underwood and R. J. So, “Can We Map Culture?” Journal of Cultural Analytics, June 2021.

T. Underwood, “Machine Learning and Human Perspective.” PMLA 135.1, January 2020, 92–109.

Relevant Projects

GPT-1914
My ongoing grant-supported project is called GPT-1914. We’re building a historical language model, and just as importantly a set of benchmarks that can be used to evaluate the performance of a model in a specific cultural context.
