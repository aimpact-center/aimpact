Bhaskar Ray Chaudhury

Assistant Professor of Industrial and Enterprise Systems Engineering
Faculty Affiliate

Website / braycha@illinois.edu

Bhaskar Ray Chaudhury is an Assistant Professor in the Department of Industrial and Enterprise Systems Engineering, also affiliated with the Siebel School of Computing and Data Science. He has worked extensively on problems in economics and computation and computational social choice with a particular emphasis on fairness. More recently, he is interested in economic paradigms (incentives, fairness, collaboration) involving data, and aims to build systems that incentivize collaborative data-sharing behavior among firms. He is also interested in topics related to human-AI collaboration and game theory for AI agents.

Published Work

On the Theoretical Foundations of Data Exchange Economies. Joint work with Hannaneh Akrami, Aniket Murhekar, and Jugal Garg. Published at EC 2025.

Human-AI Collaboration with Misaligned Preferences. Joint work with Jiaxin Song, Parnian Shahkar, and Kate Donahue. Published at EAAMO 2025.

You Get What You Give: Reciprocally Fair Federated Learning. Joint work with Aniket Murhekar, Jiaxin Song, Parnian Shahkar, and Ruta Mehta. Published at ICML 2025.

Fair Federated Learning via the Proportional Veto Core. Joint work with Aniket Murhekar, Zhuowen Yuan, Bo Li, Ruta Mehta, and Ariel D. Procaccia. Published at ICML 2024.

Incentives in Federated Learning: Equilibria, Dynamics, and Mechanisms for Welfare Maximization. Joint work with Aniket Murhekar, Zhuowen Yuan, Bo Li, and Ruta Mehta. Published at NeurIPS 2023.

Fairness in Federated Learning via Core-Stability. Joint work with Linyi Li, Mintong Kang, Bo Li, and Ruta Mehta. Published at NeurIPS 2022.

EFX Exists for Three Agents. Joint work with Kurt Mehlhorn and Jugal Garg. Published at JACM 2024.
