Ismini Lourentzou

Faculty Affiliate

Website / lourent2@illinois.edu

Ismini Lourentzou is an Assistant Professor in the School of Information Sciences at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign, where she leads the Perception and LANguage (PLAN) Lab. Her work spans multimodal machine learning, vision-language models, learning with limited supervision, and human-centric embodied AI. She has served on the NeurIPS'23 and NeurIPS'22 organizing committees and in editorial and area chair roles for PLOS Digital Health, ECCV, ACL, IEEE BigData and MICCAI. Lourentzou holds a PhD in Computer Science at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign. Before joining iSchool, she was an Assistant Professor at Virginia Tech, Computer Science Department and core faculty member of the Sanghani Center for Artificial Intelligence and Discovery Analytics, where she was received a College of Engineering 2023 Dean's Award as Outstanding New Assistant Professor.

Relevant Projects

Reasoning in Vision-Language Models: We are developing new training strategies and datasets to improve reasoning in multimodal AI, ensuring models can more reliably ground their outputs, with applications ranging from code assistants to AI4Science.

Hallucination Mitigation in AI Systems: We are building benchmarks and methods that evaluate and reduce hallucinations in vision-language models, with applications to safe AI deployment in high-stakes fields like robotics and scientific analysis.

3D AI for Embodied Interaction: We are developing part-aware 3D representations of objects to enable AI agents to understand, reason about, and interact with the physical world more effectively, which has downstream impact for robotics, training simulations, and assistive technologies.

Healthcare and Medical Imaging Applications: We design multimodal AI methods for radiology report generation and medical image analysis, , with the goal of reducing diagnostic errors and improving patient trust in automated decision support.
