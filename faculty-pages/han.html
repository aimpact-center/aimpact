Brian Han

Assistant Professor of Business Administration
Faculty Affiliate

Website / brianhan@illinois.edu

Brian Rongqing Han is an Assistant Professor of Business Administration at the Gies College of Business, University of Illinois at Urbana–Champaign. His research examines human–AI interaction in technology-enabled retail environments. Collaborating closely with industry partners such as Alibaba, he designs field experiments and analyzes large-scale data to understand how algorithmic systems transform human behavior, organizational decision-making, and market outcomes. His work has appeared in Management Science, Manufacturing & Service Operations Management, and MIS Quarterly, and has received multiple best paper and practice awards from INFORMS, ICIS, WISE, and CSAMSE. He earned his Ph.D. in Data Sciences and Operations from the USC Marshall School of Business.

Published Work

Han, B. R. & Sun, T. (Forthcoming). AI’s Invisible Hand: How Algorithms and Personal Data Shape Consumers and Competition. Management and Business Review.

This paper examines how algorithmic design and data-driven personalization reshape consumer welfare, market competition, and regulatory oversight—highlighting both the efficiency gains and equity risks associated with AI-mediated decision-making.

Relevant Projects

1. A Simple Solution to Improving Human Supervision of Algorithms: Evidence from Smart Vending
Zhao, M., Han, B. R., Chen, X., and Zhu, T. — Under Review
Examines how simple design interventions can improve human oversight of algorithmic decisions in AI-enabled retail systems.

2. Value of Autonomous Last-mile Delivery: Evidence from Alibaba
Han, B. R., Li, M., Zhang, Y., and Li, P. — Under Review
Studies how autonomous delivery robots affect consumer adoption, operational efficiency, and the role of human labor in last-mile logistics.

3. Motivating Consumer and AI Co-creation
Zhang, M., Han, B. R., Sun, T., Luo, L., and Golden, J. — Major Revision, Production and Operations Management
Investigates how platform design influences human–AI collaboration and creative participation in digital product personalization.
