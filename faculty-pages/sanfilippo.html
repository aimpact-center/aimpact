Madelyn Rose Sanfilippo

Faculty Affiliate

Website / madelyns@illinois.edu

Madelyn Rose Sanfilippo is an Assistant Professor in the School of Information Sciences at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign. Her research explores governance of sociotechnical systems and empirically supported policy addressing privacy and data protection, misinformation, and AI.

Published Work

Zhou, K. Z., Choudhry, A., Gumusel, E., & Sanfilippo, M. R. (2025). ‘Sora is incredible and scary’: public perceptions and governance challenges of text-to-video generative AI models. Information Research an international electronic journal, 30(iConf), 508-522.

Oh, S. H., & Sanfilippo, M. (2024, October). University governance for responsible AI. In Proceedings of the ALISE Annual Conference.

Relevant Projects

1. Trade secrets and proprietary AI: This project explores the application of trade secrets to AI models and training sets, often comprised of many non-proprietary or open-source components. The tensions between legal requirements and company interests are empirically examined.

2. AI ethics and governance framework applications: This project characterizes over 200 different AI ethics and governance frameworks, then empirically examines their applications in different industries, countries, and firms.
